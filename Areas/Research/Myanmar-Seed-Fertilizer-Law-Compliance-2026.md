## Key Findings

### Finding 1: Seed Law Amendment (NDSC 14/2026)

**Description**: The National Defense and Security Council enacted amendments to the Seed Law on March 7, 2026, updating the responsible ministry name and significantly increasing penalties.

**Data/Evidence**:
- **Effective Date**: March 7, 2026
- **Ministry Name Change**:
  - OLD: "Ministry of Agriculture and Irrigation"
  - NEW: "Ministry of Agriculture, Livestock and Irrigation"
- **Penalty Increases**:
  - Section 28: 1,000,000 kyats → 3,000,000 kyats (+200%)
  - Section 29: 1,000,000 kyats → 3,000,000 kyats (+200%)
- **License Requirement**: Seed business requires license from Department of Agriculture
- **Scope**: Only crops and seed classes mentioned in the license can be produced, distributed, or sold

**Implications**:
- Shwe Maha San must ensure its seed business license is valid under the new law
- License must specifically cover corn seed operations
- Non-compliance penalties are now 3x higher (3M kyats vs 1M kyats)
- All official documents referencing the ministry need updating

---

### Finding 2: Fertilizer Law Amendment (NDSC 15/2026)

**Description**: Parallel amendments to the Fertilizer Law with similar ministry name changes and penalty increases.

**Data/Evidence**:
- **Effective Date**: March 7, 2026
- **Ministry Name Change**: Same as Seed Law (added "Livestock")
- **Penalty Increases**:
  - Section 36: 1,000,000 kyats → 3,000,000 kyats (+200%)
  - Section 37: 500,000 kyats → 1,500,000 kyats (+200%)
- **License Requirement**: Fertilizer business requires license from Department of Agriculture

**Implications**:
- Shwe Pyi Feed Mill's sourcing of feed ingredients needs review
- If any fertilizer products are handled, license verification required
- Penalty awareness is critical for compliance training
- Supply chain documentation should reflect new ministry name

---

### Finding 3: License Requirements & Application Process

**Description**: Myanmar operates a formal licensing system for seed and fertilizer businesses through the Department of Agriculture.

**Data/Evidence**:
- **Licensing Authority**: Department of Agriculture under MOALI
- **Application Method**: Online through MAIRS (Myanmar Agriculture Information and Resources System)
- **Scope Restrictions**: License specifies exact crops and seed classes allowed
- **Renewal**: License validity period not specified in public sources (requires internal verification)

**Implications**:
- Shwe Maha San must hold valid license specifically covering corn seed
- License scope must match actual operations (production, distribution, sales)
- Any expansion to new seed varieties requires license amendment
- MAIRS portal account needed for renewals and updates

---

### Finding 4: Export Certificate Requirements (Thailand)

**Description**: Corn exports from Myanmar to Thailand require smog-free certificates and phytosanitary documentation.

**Data/Evidence**:
- **Smog-Free Certificate**: Required for agricultural product exports to Thailand
- **Issuing Authority**: Myanmar Department of Agriculture
- **Purpose**: Verifies products meet environmental and quality standards
- **Thailand Import Standards**: Must comply with Thai Department of Agriculture regulations

**Implications**:
- Shwe Maha San's corn exports to Thailand require current certificates
- Certificate process should be documented in standard operating procedures
- Any delays in certificate issuance impact export timelines
- Relationship with Department of Agriculture is critical
