### Vision & Strategic Intent

GT21 Holding is the Myanmar expansion vehicle of VCF Group, Thailand's leading integrated pork producer. The company's strategy is to build a full-spectrum agribusiness ecosystem in Myanmar, leveraging Thailand's operational expertise and supply chain capabilities to capture growing domestic demand in the Myanmar market.

The portfolio spans **five strategic pillars**:
- **Feed Production** — Industrial-scale animal feed manufacturing
- **Grain Sourcing & Drying** — Domestic corn procurement infrastructure
- **Livestock Farming** — Commercial fattening and breeding operations
- **Bioenergy** — Ethanol production from agricultural feedstocks
- **Dairy** — Fresh milk production for domestic consumption

### 2.1 Myanmar Feed Mill — Shwe Pyi Feed Mill

| Field | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| **Entity Name** | Shwe Pyi Feed Mill Co., Ltd. |
| **Location** | Myanmar |
| **Capacity** | 60 tonnes/hour (industrial scale) |
| **Target Commissioning** | October 2026 |
| **Project End Date** | March 2026 (Civil & Construction Phase) |
| **Current Status** | WIP — Construction & Engineering Procurement |

**Objective**: Construct and commission a high-capacity, automated animal feed mill in Myanmar to supply domestic livestock farms including GT21's own Fattening Farm (Shwe Wat) and external commercial customers. The plant will replicate VCF Group Thailand's feed mill expertise and formulation capabilities.

**Key Scope of Work**:
- Civil and structural construction of main mill building and auxiliary structures
- Procurement and installation of milling equipment (hammer mills, mixers, pelletizers)
- Grain receiving, storage, and handling systems (silos, conveying)
- Electrical, automation (PLC/SCADA), and utility systems
- Design of milling process section
- Quality control laboratory setup
- Staff training and commissioning

**Strategic Significance**: The feed mill is the anchor project of GT21's Myanmar operations — providing low-cost, locally manufactured feed to support the fattening farm and potential contract farming customers. Domestic feed production reduces reliance on Thai imports and significantly improves farm economics.

| Field | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| **Entity Name** | SinghaThong Co., Ltd. |
| **Project Type** | Industrial Bioenergy — Ethanol Production Plant |
| **Feedstock** | Agricultural feedstocks (cassava, molasses, corn) |
| **Target Completion** | March 2027 |
| **Current Status** | WIP — Construction Phase (Foundation & Fire Fighting) |

**Objective**: Build a commercial-scale ethanol production plant in Myanmar utilizing locally sourced agricultural feedstocks. The plant will produce ethanol for industrial/fuel use and potentially pharmaceutical grade alcohol.

**Current Construction Activities (Q1 2026)**:
- Piling & Concrete Pile Installation — Foundation work completed/in progress
- Fire Fighting System — Planning and construction of plant fire suppression systems
- Structural steel and civil works progressing in parallel with equipment procurement

**Strategic Significance**: Ethanol production diversifies GT21's revenue beyond feed and livestock into bioenergy, creates a demand sink for locally grown corn and cassava, and positions GT21 as a vertically integrated agro-industrial group in Myanmar. Creates synergies with the Feed Mill (distillers grains as feed ingredient) and the Corn Drying Factory.

### 2.3 Corn Drying Factory — Shwe Maha San

| Field | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| **Entity Name** | Shwe Maha San Co., Ltd. |
| **Product** | Dried corn — for feed mill input & commercial sale; corn seed supply |
| **Target Completion** | March 2026 |
| **Current Status** | WIP |

**Objective**: Establish a corn drying and storage facility to procure fresh corn from local farmers, dry it to appropriate moisture levels, and supply dried corn to Shwe Pyi Feed Mill and for commercial trading.

**Key Functions**:
- Corn procurement from local farmer network (contract / spot buying)
- Post-harvest drying using industrial continuous-flow grain dryers
- Storage silos and warehouse for buffered supply
- Corn seed distribution to contracted farmers
- Quality grading and moisture testing

**Strategic Significance**: Closes a critical gap in GT21's Myanmar supply chain — ensuring the feed mill has a stable, cost-competitive domestic corn supply year-round. Supports local farmers through seed provision and guaranteed offtake.

### 2.4 Fattening Pig Farm — Shwe Wat (MorBee)

| Field | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| **Entity Name** | Shwe Wat Farm Co., Ltd. |
| **Farm Scale** | 18 barns x 1,100 pigs per barn + Sow Farm |
| **Total Capacity** | ~19,800 fattening pigs per cycle |
| **Location** | MorBee area, Myanmar |
| **Target Completion** | April 2026 |
| **Current Status** | WIP — Barn construction; Biogas system in design |

**Objective**: Develop a commercial-scale pig fattening operation in Myanmar integrating VCF Group's world-class farm management practices. The farm will receive weanlings (from a dedicated Sow Farm) and raise them to market weight using feed exclusively supplied from Shwe Pyi Feed Mill.

**Key Features**:
- 18 fattening barns designed to VCF Group standards (ventilation, biosecurity, climate control)
- Integrated Sow Farm — closed herd system for disease control and genetics management
- Biogas System — converting farm waste into energy (Drawing stage per Q1 2026)
- Farm Flow Software integration for digital monitoring (contracts, weight gain, health records)
- Full traceability from farrowing to market weight

**Strategic Significance**: The primary revenue-generating operation in Myanmar. Creates captive demand for Shwe Pyi Feed Mill output. Leverages VCF Group's proven production protocols, genetic supply, and veterinary expertise from Thailand.
