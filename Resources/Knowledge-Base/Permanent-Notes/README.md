# Permanent Notes

## Purpose

The core of your knowledge base. These are atomic, evergreen notes that contain well-developed ideas in your own words.

## Characteristics of Good Permanent Notes

### Atomic
- One idea per note
- Focused and specific
- Easy to link and combine

### Autonomous
- Understandable on its own
- Complete thought
- No missing context

### Connected
- Links to related concepts
- Part of a network
- Value comes from connections

### Evergreen
- Timeless content
- Updated rather than duplicated
- Written for your future self

## How to Write Permanent Notes

1. **Start with a concept** from fleeting or literature notes
2. **Write in your own words** - Don't copy/paste
3. **Make it standalone** - Could you understand it in 5 years?
4. **Add connections** - Link to related notes
5. **Use clear titles** - Descriptive of the core concept

## Naming Convention

Good names:
- `habit-formation-requires-environmental-design.md`
- `compound-interest-applies-to-learning.md`
- `feedback-loops-in-complex-systems.md`

Avoid:
- `note1.md`
- `thoughts-on-habits.md`
- `2026-03-11-idea.md`

## Template

```markdown
---
title: Descriptive Concept Title
created: 2026-03-11
type: permanent
tags: [tag1, tag2]
---

# Concept Title

Clear explanation of the concept in your own words.

## Examples

Concrete examples that illustrate the concept.

## Applications

How this concept applies to your life or work.

## Connections

Related concepts:
- [[related-concept-1]]
- [[related-concept-2]]

This concept is similar to [[similar-concept]] but differs in...
This concept builds on [[foundational-concept]].

## Sources

Based on insights from:
- [[literature-note-1]]
- Personal experience
```
