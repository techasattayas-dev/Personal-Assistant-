# Yearly Reviews

## Purpose

Yearly reviews are your big-picture strategic planning time. This is when you:
- Review the entire year's progress
- Assess major life goals
- Celebrate achievements
- Learn from failures
- Plan the next year's direction

## Schedule

**Day**: December 31st or January 1st
**Time**: When you have 2-4 hours uninterrupted
**Duration**: 2-4 hours

## File Naming

Files are named by year: `YYYY.md`

Example: `2026.md`

## Yearly Review Structure

### 1. Year in Review (60 minutes)
- Major life events
- Significant achievements
- Challenges faced
- Lessons learned
- Personal growth

### 2. Financial Year in Review (30 minutes)
- Total income vs expenses
- Savings and investment growth
- Major purchases
- Financial goal achievement
- Net worth change

### 3. Area Assessment (45 minutes)
Review each Area for the entire year:
- What progress was made?
- What goals were achieved?
- What didn't work?
- What to continue/start/stop?

### 4. Project Review (15 minutes)
- Total projects completed
- Success rate
- Time management lessons
- Project selection insights

### 5. System Review (15 minutes)
- How well did the system work?
- What improvements are needed?
- Usage patterns
- Habit consistency

### 6. Next Year Planning (45 minutes)
- Set theme for next year
- Define major goals
- Plan quarterly focus areas
- Identify key projects
- Set intentions

### 7. Reflection & Gratitude (30 minutes)
- Deep reflection on growth
- Gratitude for the year
- Vision for future
- Personal insights

## Yearly Review Questions

### Looking Back

1. What were my biggest wins this year?
2. What were my biggest challenges?
3. What did I learn about myself?
4. What relationships were most meaningful?
5. What am I most proud of?
6. What would I do differently?
7. What habits served me well?
8. What habits held me back?

### Looking Forward

1. What do I want to achieve next year?
2. What kind of person do I want to become?
3. What habits do I want to build?
4. What do I want to learn?
5. What relationships do I want to nurture?
6. What impact do I want to make?
7. What experiences do I want to have?
8. What do I want to let go of?

## Tips for Deep Reflection

1. **Find quiet time** - No interruptions for 2-4 hours
2. **Review your archives** - Look at monthly reviews, completed projects
3. **Be honest** - Face both wins and failures
4. **Look for patterns** - What themes emerged?
5. **Celebrate wins** - Give yourself credit
6. **Learn from failures** - What would you do differently?
7. **Vision forward** - Where do you want to be in 1, 5, 10 years?

## Year Theme Examples

- 2026: "Year of Foundation" (building systems)
- 2027: "Year of Growth" (expanding capabilities)
- 2028: "Year of Impact" (making a difference)
- 2029: "Year of Balance" (integrating all areas)

A theme provides direction without rigid goals.
